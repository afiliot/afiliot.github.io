Biography
=========

Hi, I'm Alexandre. I work as a data scientist in the [Include](https://include-project.chru-lille.fr/en/) team of the Lille University Hospital. Include is the Integration Centre of the Lille University Hospital for Data Exploration - long story short, a data wharehouse created in 2018, which was authorized by the CNIL to reuse patients' data (_e.g_ routine medical records) for clinical and methodological research. The team is made up of two sides : data engineers that make the data available for researchers in a GDRP-friendly manner, and some data scientists that offer statistical support for short to long-term projects. Due to the rich scientific environment available on the campus, we collaborate with various clinical research teams – but also teams from Inria, Inserm and the University of Lille, to explore the potential of artificial intelligence techniques in healthcare.

As part of a small structure, I'm currently working on a broad range of subjects, from traditional statistics and machine learning techniques, _e.g._ clustering, survival analysis, time series analysis, to more advanced and specialized topics such as **deep learning analysis of histological images** - supervising another engineer, and decentralized machine learning applied to medical research thanks to the collaboration with the Inria Magnet team. 

On the occasion of the University degree organised by the University of Lille (Intelligence Artificial for Healthcare), I provided practical sessions on medical image analysis using deep learning (GitHub [here](https://github.com/afiliot/TPDUIA), french version). I also gave an opening lecture to some second-year students from Centrale Lille engineering school.
